Paula Bramwell

Clinical Psychologist | BA (Hons) MPsych (Clinical)

I will help you to explore and resolve problems connected with:

  • feeling persistently down, depressed, tired, worthless, guilty and hopeless (depression)
  • feeling anxious in social situations and being fearful that others will judge you negatively – whether it’s public speaking or simply dining out (social phobia)
  • panic attacks and fear of situations where panic may occur or where escape might be difficult such as planes, movie theatres, shopping centres, driving or catching trains or buses (panic disorder with/without agoraphobia))
  • drug and alcohol use and addictions
  • feelings of loss and grief
  • troubling memories about things from your past (trauma or abuse)
  • flashbacks or trauma which can occur after experiencing or witnessing something distressing like when someone is badly hurt, for example in an accident (post traumatic stress disorder or acute stress disorder)
  • anger and problems it can cause in your relationships with family, at work and in day-to-day interactions.

I completed my Masters in Clinical Psychology in 2006, and am a registered Clinical Psychologist. I help adults, adolescents and children (from the age of fourteen and up) to overcome complex issues.

I approach our conversations with empathy, warmth and understanding, and use evidence-based interventions to help you resolve challenges to your mental health. I provide a space where you can safely explore and understand difficult experiences, thoughts and feelings.

I will work together with you to help you realise your goals.
